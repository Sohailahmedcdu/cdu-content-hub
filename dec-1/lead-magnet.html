📋 Export Business Startup Checklist

Essential steps to launch your car export operation

Legal & Business Setup

  • Research federal vs provincial incorporation (costs $150-$300)
  • File articles of incorporation
  • Obtain business license from local municipality
  • Apply for OMVIC wholesale/export license (Ontario)
  • Prepare business plan (5-10 pages)
  • Open business bank account (bring docs)
  • Register for HST if revenue will exceed $30K/year
  • Obtain Director/Officer ID from OMVIC

Financial Setup

  • Determine startup capital needed ($5K-$25K minimum)
  • Set up accounting system (QuickBooks or similar)
  • Plan for HST obligations (13% on sales)
  • Calculate commission structure for first deals
  • Set aside funds for vehicle inspections ($200-$500 per car)
  • Research insurance requirements (dealer liability)
  • Plan for shipping costs (quote several providers)

Technology & Operations

  • Subscribe to Signal software (vehicle appraisal)
  • Set up business email and website
  • Create social media accounts (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok)
  • Install CRM system (Pipedrive, GoHighLevel, etc.)
  • Create inventory tracking spreadsheet
  • Set up document storage (Google Drive, OneDrive)
  • Research payment gateway for online deals

Market Research

  • Identify target market (Middle East, Africa, USA)
  • Research vehicle preferences by market
  • Study competitor pricing and margins
  • Build list of potential buyers
  • Research freight companies and shipping costs
  • Understand import regulations in target countries
  • Calculate break-even pricing for different vehicles
💡 Pro Tip: Don't purchase your first vehicle until you have a buyer lined up. This reduces risk and ensures profitability from day one.

First Deal Preparation

  • Source 3-5 vehicle options for your target market
  • Get pre-purchase inspection from trusted mechanic
  • Verify clean title and no liens
  • Calculate all-in cost (purchase + inspection + shipping + HST)
  • Confirm buyer is legitimate and has funds
  • Get shipping quote from 2-3 freight companies
  • Document all agreements in writing
  • Complete deal and track for profitability analysis

Ready to launch your export business?

Common Startup Timeline

Week 1-2: Incorporate, apply for licenses

Week 2-3: Open bank account, set up systems

Week 3-4: Research market, build buyer network

Week 4-6: Source first vehicles, close first deals

Ongoing: Scale to 2-3 deals/month
